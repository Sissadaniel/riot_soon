Location

With a sea whose waters go from intense blue to brilliant green and beaches of fine white sand, this town should be your first stop in getting to know the beautiful Caribbean Sea.

Imagine a simple, quiet fishing village, with dishes prepared from the best that the sea has to offer, and one of the most spectacular reefs for learning how to dive ... this is Puerto Morelos.

Among its attractions are a historic lighthouse, a botanical garden, and several cenotes with turquoise water and ceilings covered in stalactites and hanging plants.

What is FuckUp Riot?

FuckUp Riot is the combination of two events: the first global gathering of FuckUp Nights organizers “Fuckupers”, and the 5th Anniversary celebration of the movement.

When and where is the event?

September 20-24, 2017 in Puerto Morelos, Mexico.

What will be the schedule of activities during the FuckUp Riot?

If you want more information about the FuckUp Riot agenda, check out this link

When should I arrive?

We recommend arriving on the 19th so that you will feel rested and ready to start the day of activities on the 20th. However, your hotel reservations in the package start on the night of the 20th, so if you want to reserve the night of the 19th get in touch with us and we’ll make the arrangements: info@fuckupriot.com.

What if I want to arrive earlier or leave later?

The surrounding areas offer some incredible tourist destinations, so you are more than welcome to arrive before or leave after the event. Remember that hotel reservations, activities, and meals outside of the FuckUp Riot schedule of events will need to be paid for and organized on your own. If you would like to stay longer in the same hotel in Puerto Morelos, let us know and we will make the arrangements to extend your reservation.

Is it OK if I arrive later in the week?

It’s not a problem if you arrive late, but we recommend that you arrive on time so you don’t miss anything. Plus your input and participation is very valuable to us.

Do I need a visa to travel to Mexico?

It depends on where you’re from. Here you can find a list of countries that need a visa to enter México: www.sectur.gob.mx. If it is required for your visa, we can give you a letter of invitation and information about your accommodations. If you need anything else, please let us know.

Which is the nearest airport?

Cancun International Airport, with connections to almost all international hubs.

How do I get from Cancun Airport to Puerto Morelos?

Puerto Morelos is approximately 26 km (30 minutes) away. The best way is to pay for a taxi ($28 USD) or a shuttle service ($10 USD each).

Can my partner or friend attend the FuckUp Riot?

Yes, of course! If he or she knows about the concept of FuckUp Nights and wants to participate in the workshops and discussions he/she is welcome to buy a package.

Can I bring kids?

Of course! We only remind you that the FuckUp Riot schedule will be demanding and it may be difficult for you to keep an eye on your little ones. We recommend only bringing kids if you’re coming with someone who can help you look after them during the event.

Can my friends or partner say in the same hotel as me, even if they are not attending the FuckUp Riot?

Yes, in that case we can help you with your hotel arrangements so that you can all stay in the same hotel (subject to availability, prices may vary).

If my friends/partner want to only attend the 5th Anniversary celebration (Sep. 23rd), is that possible?

Yes, tickets for the 5th Anniversary celebration will be available to the general public.

If I am coming with my partner, which ticket package should we buy?

If you are coming with a partner/friend who will be participating in the FuckUp Riot activities, then we recommend you purchase two “Amigo” packages. Then just let us know what your preference is for sleeping arrangements (two single beds in the same room or a double bed). If you are coming with a partner that will not be participating in the activities, then you can buy a “Forever Alone” package and your partner can stay in the room with you, but without the other benefits (meals and event ticket).

Are all expenses covered by FuckUppers?

Yes. You will need to purchase one of the ticket & hotel packages that we designed for you, plus all additional expenses (flights, transportation to and from the airport, food and drink during the 5th Anniversary celebration, etc.) are covered by you. The only way to have access to the FuckUp Riot activities is to buy a package.

What type of hotel is included in the package reservation?

It is a four star hotel with basic services, including hot water, air conditioning, and WiFi. Comfortable but nothing luxurious. A few steps from the ocean.

What forms of payment are accepted for the purchase of tickets?

We accept credit/debit card and Paypal,

How much extra money should I bring?

That depends on each person and what extra activities you plan on doing outside of the event. With the ticket packages you're basically covered, but you may want to spend on extra meals and drinks, or other incidentals. Puerto Morelos is an affordable town and there are ATMs if you need to take out more money.

What if I can’t afford to attend?

Please tell us about your situation and we will consider if we can help you in any way.

Will you be streaming the FuckUp Riot live?

Yes, on Facebook live through our Facebook fan page.

What is the official language of the FuckUp Riot?

The official language is English, however there may be some activities Spanish. We have plenty of potential translators in our FuckUp community who can help translate.

If I want to give a workshop or talk, how can I make it happen?

Please get in touch with us with your proposal: info@fuckupriot.com

If I want to propose a topic for the global gathering, who do I get in touch with?

Get in touch at: info@fuckupriot.com

What type clothing should I bring?

Weather should be hot and sunny, with a small possibility of rain. So bring light clothes.

What tourist attractions are there in the area?

Do I have to worry about safety in Puerto Morelos?

Despite all the bad news that you may hear about Mexico, the Mayan Riviera is a popular international tourist destination visited by hundreds of thousands of people each year. Puerto Morelos is a calm fishing village and is quite safe.

Do I have to worry about getting sick?

As in most tropical places, there is always a possibility of illness. However chances of getting sick are quite low, and there will be medical services on hand. If you are worried, you may want to buy travel health insurance. If you take prescription medication, makes sure to bring them from your home countries.
